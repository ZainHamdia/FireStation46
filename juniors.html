Junior Firefighter Program

Training the next generation of emergency responders

About the Program

The Montgomery Township Volunteer Fire Company #2 Junior Firefighter Program offers high school students ages 16 to 18 a unique, hands-on opportunity to learn about the volunteer fire service. Our program is designed to develop leadership, teamwork, and technical skills while providing a direct path to becoming a full active-duty firefighter at age 18.

What You'll Do & Learn

As a Junior Firefighter, you will be integrated into fire company training, meetings, and non-hazardous exterior operations on emergency scenes. Under close supervision of experienced personnel, you will train on:

  • Rescue Operations: Vehicle extrication techniques, stabilization, and usage of hydraulic tools.
  • Hose & Ladder Work: Learning proper handling of attack lines, supply lines, and ground ladders.
  • Communications: Operating fire department radios and understanding emergency dispatch protocols.
  • Equipment Familiarity: Gaining experience with SCBA (self-contained breathing apparatus), thermal imaging cameras, and gas meters.

Program Requirements

  • • Age: Must be between 16 and 18 years old.
  • • Consent: Full written consent from a parent or legal guardian is required.
  • • Academics First: Must maintain a passing GPA and remain in good standing in school. Academic and family activities always take priority.
  • • Attendance: Expected to attend weekly company drills (Wednesdays at 7:00 PM) and monthly meetings when school schedules permit.

Path to Full Certification

Upon turning 18, Junior Firefighters are eligible to enroll in the **Firefighter 1** state certification course at the Somerset County Emergency Services Training Academy. The tuition, course materials, and all safety gear are fully funded by the fire company.
